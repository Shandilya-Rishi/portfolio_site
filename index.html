Hello, World! — A Little About Me →

Every great journey starts with a single step — or in the developer world, a "Hello, World!" So here's mine.

My name is Rishi Shandilya, a Computer Science freshman based in India, and this is my first blog post. I figured there's no better way to kick off this space than by introducing myself, talking about what drives me, and sharing what I'm looking forward to building.

Who Am I?

I'm someone who believes the best way to grow is to stay open — open to new experiences, new ideas, and new challenges. As a CSE freshman, I'm still early in my journey, but that's exactly what makes this phase exciting. Every concept I learn feels like a new door opening, and I'm genuinely trying to walk through as many of them as I can.

I'm not the kind of person who waits for the "right moment" to start something. If it's interesting, I explore it. If it challenges me, I lean in. That curiosity is what keeps me going.

The Tech Side: Machine Learning

If there's one area that genuinely excites me right now, it's Machine Learning. There's something almost magical about the idea of teaching a machine to learn from data and make decisions — and I'm actively pursuing that. From understanding the math behind models to experimenting with real datasets, I'm in the thick of learning and I love it.

ML feels like the frontier of what's possible in computing right now, and I want to be someone who contributes to that frontier, not just watches it from a distance.

Open Source: Building in Public

One of my upcoming goals is to get serious about open source. I want to work on my own projects and also contribute to others' — because I think that's one of the best ways to actually grow as a developer. Reading production code, collaborating with people across the world, shipping real features — that's the kind of hands-on experience no classroom can fully replicate.

If you're working on something interesting and could use a motivated contributor, feel free to reach out. I'm all in.

The Creative Side: Anime Edits

Now, here's something that might surprise you — I also watch and make anime edits. It's a creative outlet that's completely separate from code, and I think that balance matters. Editing requires a good eye for storytelling, pacing, and emotion — skills that, honestly, aren't too different from building great software experiences.

Some of my favorite series have genuinely shaped the way I think about persistence, ambition, and the grind. If you're an anime fan too, we already have a lot to talk about.

What's This Blog About?

This blog is going to be a mix of everything — technical learnings, project updates, thoughts on ML research, and maybe the occasional personal reflection. Think of it as a public journal of someone figuring things out in real time.

I don't have all the answers, and I'm not pretending to. But I do have curiosity, consistency, and a lot of keyboard time ahead of me.

Thanks for reading. This is just the beginning.

— Rishi Shandilya
